Q-Mol — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-24. Q-Mol is operated by Photon Bounce ("we", "us"). Contact: privacy@qmol.app.

This policy explains what data Q-Mol collects when you use the website, API, CLI, or mobile app, why we collect it, and your choices. We sell software, not your data.

2. How we use it

To provide and bill the service, prevent abuse, keep the service reliable, and contact you about your account. We do not use your submitted molecular structures to build or sell datasets, and we do not share them with third parties, except as needed to run the computation you requested or as required by law.

3. Your molecular structures

Structures you submit are processed to return descriptors/results. We cache derived results (keyed by a structure hash) to make repeat requests fast. For async jobs, your input and output files are stored temporarily so you can download them, then deleted. We do not sell, license, or share your submitted structures. Treat the public free tier as non-confidential; for confidential chemistry, use a paid plan or self-host.

4. Sharing / third parties

We use service providers only to operate Q-Mol: a cloud host (compute), Stripe (payments), Google Play Billing (in-app purchases on Android), and an email provider (key delivery). They process data on our behalf under their own terms. We do not sell personal data.

5. Your rights

You can access, export, rotate your key, or delete your account and associated data at any time by emailing privacy@qmol.app. Depending on your location (e.g. GDPR/CCPA) you may have additional rights to access, correct, delete, or port your data, and to object to processing.

6. Security & retention

Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS). We keep data only as long as needed for the purposes above or as required by law, then delete it.

7. Children

Q-Mol is a professional/research tool not directed to children under 13 (or the age of digital consent in your region) and we do not knowingly collect their data.

8. Changes

We'll update this page and the "last updated" date when our practices change.
